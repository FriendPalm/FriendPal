About FP

It is likely that you have written a letter to an anonymous person when you were in elementary. Back then, it was a way for us children to practice our handwriting (if we wrote it), and to also get a brief taste of conversing through writing letters. That activity you once did is a term known as pen pal. A pen pal is a person with whom one keeps up an exchange of letters, usually someone far away that a personal meeting is unlikely (Dictionary.com). The term is very broad, as it means everyone can write to anyone. But our application, FriendPal, wants to specify the pen pal term by targeting a specific demographic, college students. We feel that college students will benefit from it the most, as it is easier for students to relate to one another. College is an enormous journey for many people, for it shapes our future and helps us create bonds with people we meet through our years. But there are stages in college where meeting new people can be proven difficult. There may be a semester that you have to focus heavily on classes, which causes you to sacrifice your social life. You lose that semester’s worth of opportunities to make new friends, which can be demoralizing for some. There are times when one might say, “I wish I had someone to talk to, someone to rant with, someone to cope through the struggles of being a college student”. With our FriendPal application, college students will have the freedom to do that, either anonymously or not. When you use our app, you must create an account using your college email address. This prevents a person from creating multiple personas. It will also help the application randomly generate, based on location, a potential pen pal for you. Once an account is created, you are given options to either respond to a letter or create one yourself. Making a letter is similar to making a blog post on WordPress. You can add media to compliment your letter, and also create tags, which help other users filter their search when looking for a potential pen pal. Simple email, text messaging, and internet chat applications also do similar things, but we allow users to talk to more than just others who speak the same language as that user. We will be incorporating a translator that can be modified to help review or learn a language. Google has a translator that they incorporate into emails that automatically detects if a message is sent in a language other than your default language. There may be an instance where you (an English speaker) are paired with a student from Korea, and communication will be difficult since both users will have problems talking to each other. With our app, users will not have to worry; after writing your letter, the app will translate it, making it ready to read for students from foreign countries. The letter you write will be read in 44 different languages in countries all over the world. Patience is a trait that a few college students have, which makes waiting for a reply to your letter a bit nerve wracking. The FriendPal app can also be set to accept live chat invitations from users currently online. The app will also have the option to have as few or as many pen pals you like. You will be able to set a preference, such as the activity level you think you will have for this app. If you feel you can manage more than one pen pal, set your activity level to the max and the app sends your introduction letter to people around the world that it thinks will be compatible with you. We want it to be a universal application for every college student to use. This application will be free of cost for students, and it will only make money through plug-ins such as incorporating mail services, to send each other packages. The purpose for this application is to prevent students from feeling like they’re alone during their college careers, and to aid them by supplementing them an application that maintains the social aspect you experience in college. The hardware and software requirements to use FriendPal are quite minimal. The general hardware needed for both Android and iOS is the camera, GPS, roughly 100MBs of space. FriendPal will need access to the camera so you can document your letters, and the GPS is needed to share your location in your letters. For software requirements, FriendPal supports OS 8 or later and Android 4.4 or later.
